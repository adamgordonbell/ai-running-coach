# Dynamic Coaching Context
*Last updated: 2026-03-16*

## Body Signals
- Ankle instability; occasional hip/back tightness—prioritize mobility after travel or long days at desk.
- Left foot: mild metatarsal irritation after technical trail/root strikes; feet often sore in the first hour of treadmill LRs, then ease.
- Achilles has felt "spicy" after high-volume weeks—monitor load.
- Deep left-side glute DOMS for days after big LRs—bias recovery work the following day.
- Left knee and hip pain after long pavement runs, especially downhill—avoid steep/extended downhills on hard surfaces.
- Core is a limiter: hollow body holds and roll-ups are very hard—keep regressions in play.
- Blood sugar: small carb snack within 10 min post-run. During LR: electrolytes + maple syrup work well.
- Avoid alcohol around big sessions—worsens sleep and next-day dehydration.
- ADHD medication boosts morning motivation and can raise BP; recent readings normal.

## Motivators
- Long runs: movies make time fly. Keep a 3-film watchlist ready; browse during warm-up if nothing's queued.
- Weeknight Z2: a running series lowers starting friction.
- Treadmill coding: pairing runs with work tasks keeps engagement high.
- Headphone-free outdoor runs → good creative/reflective time.
- Social runs with a training partner are motivating but tend toward Z3/Z4.
- EF benchmarks (local trail races): you finish 10k at HR 163, EF 0.89. Training partner: EF 1.09. Top finishers: EF ~1.21. Near-term goal: reach 1.0+ at race effort.
- When momentum feels low: a simple plan (show queued + 45–60 min Z2) beats a perfect plan.

## Schedule & Preferences
- Weeknights: 17:00 shutdown & go (slot A), or after dinner 19:30–21:00 (slot B).
- Weekend LR: 09:30–12:30. Lock in day/time by Friday.
- Weekly targets: ~5 h aerobic (LR included) + 1× strength. If 5 h hit early, swap a run for mobility or Z1.
- Strength (Peloton Andy Speer): RPE 6–7, skip failure, prioritize hinge and core. On run-heavy weeks, pick upper-body classes to spare legs.
- Don't stack strength and long runs on the same day—glute DOMS compounds badly.

## Discoveries
- Morning runs feel easier than after-work Z2—schedule big aerobic in the AM when possible.
- Z2 treadmill benchmark: 2% incline at speed 4 sustainable for ~2 h. Retest monthly.
- Technical singletrack makes strict Z2 nearly impossible—pick less technical routes when zone discipline matters.
- Coding LRs accumulate volume well but blur progress signals—include one continuous run per week.
- Hiking works as Z1 volume when travel disrupts normal training.
